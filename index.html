Quote & payment — New Customer

Customer contacts Pivot to request a quote. A rep builds it in HubSpot and sends a payment request. Customer pays by credit card, ACH, or PO/invoice. Learning access is not blocked by payment status — but scheduler access is gated until payment is confirmed.

Pivot Subscription Transition

Existing existing customers transitioning to Pivot are offered an incentive discount to switch to the Pivot subscription model immediately. All other existing customers are required to transition at their next recertification cycle. Once transitioned, the experience from Phase 2 onward is identical to a new customer.

Select a section:
CSM identifies existing Pivot account→Outreach sent→Transition offer presented→Customer elects immediate or at-recert transition

Incentive offer — immediate transition

  • Existing existing customers transitioning to Pivot are offered a discount incentive to transition to the Pivot subscription model immediately rather than waiting for their recertification cycle
  • CSM presents the offer directly — outreach via email and follow-up call
  • Incentive pricing is reflected in the HubSpot quote
  • Customers who accept transition immediately: account is migrated, new subscription activated, legacy previous platform access is closed

Required transition — at recertification

  • Customers who do not accept the immediate offer are required to transition to the subscription model at their next recertification cycle
  • CSM documents the transition date in HubSpot based on the customer's previous recertification schedule
  • Customer continues on Pivot until that date — no disruption to active certifications
  • CSM re-engages 60 days before the recert date to begin the transition process
HubSpot — transition status and timeline on Company recordCSM — outreach and offer management
Rep pulls Pivot account record→Determines recertification status for all users→Applies correct scenario pricing→Quote generated

Pricing depends on where users are in their certification cycle. Every existing customer falls into one of four scenarios — or a combination. The CSM reviews the Pivot account record and identifies which scenario applies before generating the quote.

Scenario 1 — All users recertified this year

  • License cost: No Year 1 license cost — users are already certified for the current calendar year
  • Implementation fee: Charged
  • Instructor access: Instructors get access to new Pivot materials immediately upon transition
  • Practitioner access: Practitioners gain access to new Pivot materials on January 1 when the subscription formally kicks in
  • Subscription renews at standard pricing at the start of the next calendar year

Scenario 2A — No users recertified; seats purchased but unused

  • License cost: Standard pricing
  • Prorated discount: Applied based on time remaining in the calendar year
  • Implementation fee: Charged
  • Credit: Cost of unused previous licenses subtracted from the Pivot subscription invoice
  • Users begin the Pivot certification process upon transition — no recertification credit carried over

Scenario 2B — No users recertified; seats not yet purchased

  • License cost: Standard pricing
  • Prorated discount: Applied based on time remaining in the calendar year
  • Implementation fee: Charged
  • No credit applied — no prior Pivot payment to offset
  • Users begin the Pivot certification process upon transition

Scenario 3 — Mixed: some recertified, some not

  • Implementation fee: Charged
  • Already recertified users: No Year 1 license cost
  • Not yet recertified users: Standard pricing + prorated discount
  • Credits: Applied for any unused Pivot seats
  • Instructor access: All Instructors get access to new Pivot materials immediately
  • Practitioners already certified: Access to new materials on January 1 when subscription costs kick in
  • Practitioners not yet recertified: Access to new materials upon recertification

Scenario 4 — Adding net new users

  • Applies when the organization wants to add staff who were not part of the Pivot account
  • License cost: Standard pricing + prorated discount based on time remaining in the calendar year
  • Net new users begin the Pivot certification process from the start — no prior certification to honor
  • Can be added at transition or at any point during the subscription year
  • CSM generates a quote and payment link for net new seats

What the implementation fee covers

  • Account setup and migration of prior certification records to Pivot HubSpot
  • Certification mapping (prior certification tiers → Pivot tiers) — recorded on each Contact record
  • Catalog and Canvas provisioning
  • CSM kickoff call and Adoption Toolkit delivery
  • Charged once at transition regardless of scenario — not repeated at renewal

Scheduling the skills lab

After payment is confirmed, institutional admin(s) with an Instructor subscription gain access to the scheduling tool. Associates and Practitioners do not perform any scheduling — their participation is arranged by the institutional admin(s). Two paths: host a training at your own location, or join an existing nearby training.

Select a section:

Access gate: Scheduling is only accessible to institutional admin(s) with an Instructor tier in their subscription. Associates and Practitioners do not perform any scheduling activities — their participation in the skills lab is arranged by the institutional admin(s) on their behalf.

What triggers scheduler access

  • For CC / ACH: Authorize.net confirms payment → HubSpot automatically sets payment confirmed flag
  • For PO / invoice: HubSpot workflow triggers payment confirmed flag automatically on receipt of payment
  • Account lockout for overdue accounts is automated — access suspended when payment is overdue per defined thresholds
  • Once payment confirmed flag is set, institutional admin(s) can access the scheduling tool

Two scheduling paths available

  • Path A — Host a Training: Institutional admin(s) provide the venue, choose a 3-day window, and optionally open the event to nearby orgs
  • Path B — Join a Training: Institutional admin(s) browse already-scheduled nearby trainings and register their staff into available seats
  • Both paths use the same Training Event object in HubSpot
  • Associates and Practitioners are registered by the institutional admin(s) — they do not access the scheduler directly
Admin selects "Host a Training"→Chooses 3-day window→Trainer availability checked→Event created in HubSpot→Synced to Google Calendar→Opened to nearby orgs

What the institutional admin(s) do

  • Accesses scheduling tool (payment confirmed gate cleared)
  • Signs the Training Site Agreement before the event is confirmed — this is a separate agreement from the subscription contract, specific to the physical training location and event
  • Selects "Host a Training" and provides venue details
  • Views calendar showing valid trainer availability windows
  • Selects preferred 3-day window from available options
  • Registers all attendees (Associates, Practitioners, and Instructors) into the event in HubSpot — Catalog is not used for event scheduling
  • Can request the event be marked private if at least 10 people are registered — private events are not visible to nearby institutions

What happens in the system

  • Custom API validates trainer availability against Google Calendar
  • Training Event object created in HubSpot with all details
  • Event synced to Google Calendar for the assigned trainer
  • Event marked as "Open / Shared" by default — or "Private" if requested and 10+ registrants are confirmed
  • Capacity tracked in HubSpot — no Catalog involvement in event scheduling
HubSpot — Training Event objectGoogle Calendar — trainer schedulingCustom API — availability validation

System roles

  • Google Calendar: trainer availability engine and calendar execution
  • Custom APIs: validate availability, create events, sync between HubSpot and Google Calendar
  • Catalog: seat management — not event scheduling

Training mats — institution responsibility

  • Institutions hosting a training must purchase their own training mats before the event
  • Pivot provides a list of preferred vendors — institutions source and purchase independently
  • Mats are not provided or shipped by Pivot
  • Confirm mat requirements with Pivot rep when scheduling

Curriculum manual — Instructors

  • Each Instructor receives a physical copy and a digital copy of the Pivot curriculum manual
  • Physical copy is shipped to the onsite training location and distributed to Instructors during the skills lab
  • Physical manuals are also available for purchase for those who need additional or replacement copies
  • Digital copy accessible within Canvas Career at any time
  • Associates and Practitioners receive digital access in Canvas only — no physical manual
  • Manual is licensed to the individual — not to be copied or distributed

All other materials — Canvas + print

  • All supporting materials made available within Canvas Career
  • Accessible at any time during the active subscription year
  • Materials can be downloaded and printed if desired
  • Digital access covers all tiers: Associate, Practitioner, and Instructor

Materials summary by tier

  • Practitioner: Digital materials in Canvas (printable)
  • Instructor: Physical curriculum manual (shipped to onsite training location, distributed at skills lab; also available for purchase) + digital manual in Canvas + all other digital materials in Canvas (printable)
  • Hosting institution: Must procure training mats from Pivot-preferred vendors

Certificate and badge are one credential. Badgr Pro is used to design and issue the certificate in badge format. The learner receives a single Badgr Pro credential that serves as both their digital badge and their certificate — shareable anywhere, verifiable, and tied to their permanent Badgr profile.

The Badgr Pro credential

  • Awarded automatically when all Catalog program requirements are met — no manual step
  • Learner receives an email notification to claim their credential from Badgr Pro
  • Serves as both the digital badge and the certificate — one credential
  • Shareable to LinkedIn, email signature, digital portfolio
  • Credential metadata: tier, issuing org (Pivot), learner name, issue date, credential year
  • Learner owns their Badgr profile — persists independently of employer
  • Verifiable URL for each issued credential

Admin notification + HubSpot data push

  • Admin is notified each time a credential is issued for a learner in their institution
  • Badgr Pro pushes credential award data to HubSpot automatically
  • HubSpot certification record updated: status set to "Certified", credential ID recorded
